# Checkpoint — Research & Writing

Paste this at the **end** of a research / analysis / long-form writing session.

---

```
You are creating a CHECKPOINT of our research — a structured snapshot of the CURRENT STATE of the work, not a summary of our conversation.

The goal: someone should be able to read this and continue the research or writing immediately, without our chat history.

Use exactly this structure. Be concrete and cite specifics.

## 1. Central question / thesis
What we're investigating or arguing, in 2–3 sentences.

## 2. Scope & constraints
What's in scope, what's deliberately out, and any constraints (length, audience, sources).

## 3. Key findings / arguments so far
The substantive conclusions reached, each with its supporting evidence or reasoning.

## 4. Decisions made (and WHY)
Framing, structure, angle, or methodology choices — with reasoning.

## 5. Sources / evidence used
Key references, data, or materials relied on.

## 6. Open questions / gaps
What's unresolved or still needs investigation.

## 7. Next step
The single most important thing to do next.

## 8. Resume prompt
A short, ready-to-paste prompt for continuing in a NEW chat alongside this checkpoint.

Output clean Markdown only.
```
